Brief Bio.

Kimio Kuramitsu leads the Software Assurance research group at Yokohama National University. His work focuses broadly on data engineering, ubiquitous and dependable computing, and programming language design. He has received the Yamashita Research Award at IPSJ, and grants from Japan Society for the Promotion of Science, Information Processing Agency, and Japan Science and Technology Agency, including the JST/CREST research director. His pedagogical achievements include ASPEN, the first C programming excecise environment for students. Prof. Kimio earned his earned his B.E. at the University of Tokyo, and his Ph.D. at the University of Tokyo, under the supervision of Prof. Ken Sakamura.
